ðŸš— Introduction to Vehicle Insurance

Vehicle insurance is a contractual agreement between a vehicle owner and an insurance company that provides financial protection against losses resulting from accidents, theft, natural disasters, or other unforeseen events. It is a legal requirement in most countries, ensuring that drivers can cover the costs of damage or injury caused to others. The primary goal of vehicle insurance is to mitigate financial risks and promote responsible driving behavior.

ðŸ“œ Types of Vehicle Insurance Coverage

There are several types of vehicle insurance policies, each offering different levels of protection:

  • Third-Party Liability Insurance: Basic and mandatory coverage protecting against third-party claims.
  • Comprehensive Insurance: Covers third-party liabilities and own vehicle damages from accidents, theft, and disasters.
  • Collision Coverage: Pays for damage caused by collisions, regardless of fault.
  • Personal Accident Cover: Compensation for driver and passengers in case of injury or death.
  • Zero Depreciation Cover: Ensures full replacement cost without depreciation deductions.
